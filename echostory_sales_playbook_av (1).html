EchoStorySales Playbook

This offer is not “a webpage with music.” It is a fully custom digital gift experience built around one person. The customer is buying emotion, personalization, presentation, and a moment they can share.

What the customer is actually buying

The real offer

  • Custom-written songs using names, hobbies, memories, and direct messages.
  • Custom story arc and themed presentation.
  • Interactive reveal pages, music players, and gift-delivery flow.
  • Custom art direction and page styling around the recipient.

What to avoid saying

  • Do not call it a “simple HTML page.”
  • Do not frame it like a generic music player.
  • Do not lead with technical features before emotional value.
  • Do not discount too early.
Best framing: “We create a personalized digital gift world around the person you care about — with custom songs, custom story, custom visuals, and an interactive reveal experience.”

Commission structure for a commission-only team

Because this is a commission-only role, commission needs to be meaningful enough to reward prospecting, pitching, follow-up, and closing effort.

Deal Tier Rep Commission How to use it
Full-price close 20% Primary target. Protect this whenever possible.
Small courtesy discount 17% Use only when a buyer is serious but hesitating on timing.
Moderate save-the-sale discount 14% Use only with clear buying signals and real price resistance.
Last-step rescue discount 10% Manager-approved closer. Use sparingly to preserve value.
Deep discount / exception 5% Rare emergency use only. Better to hold price than live here.

How to defend price

Anchor to emotion

“This is built around one person. The songs, story, visuals, and reveal are all customized. It is closer to commissioning a keepsake than buying a generic website.”

Anchor to originality

“This is not a template with a name dropped in. It is custom lyrics, custom structure, custom presentation, and a delivery experience built around the relationship.”

Anchor to memory value

“People forget links. They remember moments. This is designed to feel like opening a gift, not receiving a file.”

Anchor to scope

“There are multiple creative disciplines inside this: writing, personalization, song concepting, page design, interactive build, and final delivery polish.”

When to use discounts

Use a discount when

  • The buyer clearly wants it but is stuck on budget.
  • The occasion is time-sensitive and a smaller concession closes now.
  • The buyer is comparing this to another gift option and needs a push.

Do not use a discount when

  • The rep has not yet fully explained the value.
  • The buyer is just browsing.
  • The buyer has not shared enough intent signals to justify a concession.
Rule of thumb: defend value first, discount second, and only discount with a purpose. Every step down should feel earned.

Easy pitch flow

1. Open

“This is a fully custom digital gift experience built around the person you’re buying for.”

2. Personalize

Ask about the recipient’s name, hobbies, relationship, occasion, favorite things, and emotional tone.

3. Paint the picture

Describe the reveal moment, the custom songs, the story world, and how the person will feel opening it.

4. Price confidently

Present Deluxe first. That is the main value package and easiest range to defend.
